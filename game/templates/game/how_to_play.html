How to Play Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe

Basic Concept

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe is a more complex version of classic Tic-Tac-Toe, consisting of 9 smaller Tic-Tac-Toe boards arranged in a 3×3 grid (making a total 9×9 play area).

Game Rules

  1. Board Structure: There are 9 small 3×3 boards that make up one large 3×3 board.
  2. First Move: The first player can place their mark (X or O) in any small square of any small board.
  3. Subsequent Moves:
    • Wherever you place your mark determines which small board the next player must play in
    • For example, if you play in the center square of a small board, your opponent must play in the center small board of the large grid
  4. Winning Small Boards:
    • Win a small board by getting 3 in a row (like regular Tic-Tac-Toe)
    • Once a small board is won, it's marked as belonging to that player in the large grid
  5. Forced Boards: If you're sent to a small board that's already won or full, you get to play in any other small board of your choice.
  6. Winning the Game: Win the ultimate game by winning 3 small boards in a row on the large grid (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).

Special Cases

  • If a small board ends in a draw, it counts for neither player in the large grid
  • If the entire game ends with no player achieving 3 small boards in a row, the winner is determined by who won the most small boards
